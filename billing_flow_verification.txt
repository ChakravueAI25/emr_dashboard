BILLING FLOW VERIFICATION REPORT
Repository inspected: backend/main.py, backend/database.py
Scope: analysis only. No application code was changed.

====================================================================
1. SUMMARY
====================================================================

The billing architecture in backend/main.py is hybrid and inconsistent.

There are two active storage patterns:
1. Patient-embedded billing data in patients.billing.* arrays.
2. Standalone billing collections such as billing_invoices, initial_surgery_bills, final_surgery_bills, billing_cases, and pharmacy_billing.

The main issue is not just that billing is hybrid. The issue is that different endpoints write to different storage models, while most read endpoints only read one side of that storage. As a result, billing data can be created successfully but remain invisible to the dashboard or patient billing APIs depending on which endpoint was used.

Key conclusion:
- Core patient invoice, payment, claim, and surgery-bill APIs mostly use patients.billing arrays.
- Alternate invoice and dated surgery-bill APIs use standalone collections.
- Pharmacy billing writes both to pharmacy_billing and to a mirrored patient.pharmacyBills array.
- Billing dashboard and billing analytics do not read billing_invoices, initial_surgery_bills, or final_surgery_bills.
- There is no single source of truth for billing.

4.2 Aggregation pipelines used

Pipeline 1: invoice_pipeline on patients
Stages:
- $unwind $billing.invoices
- $group totals for:
  - totalRevenue: sum patientResponsibility where status = paid
  - pendingBills: count where status != paid
  - refunds: sum patientResponsibility where status = refunded

Pipeline 2: surgery_pipeline on patients
Stages:
- $unwind $billing.surgeryBills
- $group totals for:
  - totalRevenue: sum patientTotalShare where status in [settled, balance_due, partially_paid]
  - settledRevenue: sum patientTotalShare where status = settled
  - unsettledRevenue: sum patientTotalShare where status in [balance_due, partially_paid]
  - pendingBills: count where status = balance_due
  - refunds: sum refundAmount

Pipeline 3: pharmacy_pipeline on pharmacy_billing
Stages:
- $group totals for:
  - totalRevenue: sum totalAmount where status = completed
  - pendingBills: count where status != completed

Pipeline 4: pending invoices hover-card pipeline on patients
Stages:
- $unwind $billing.invoices
- $match status != paid
- $sort by invoice date descending
- $limit 5
- $project patient name, registrationId, invoice

Pipeline 5: pending surgery hover-card pipeline on patients
Stages:
- $unwind $billing.surgeryBills
- $match status != settled
- $sort by createdAt descending
- $limit 5
- $project patient name, registrationId, bill

Pipeline 6: recent invoices pipeline on patients
Stages:
- $unwind $billing.invoices
- $sort by invoice date descending
- $limit limit
- $project patient identity and invoice

Pipeline 7: recent surgery pipeline on patients
Stages:
- $unwind $billing.surgeryBills
- $sort by createdAt descending
- $limit limit
- $project patient identity and bill

Non-aggregation collection reads:
- pending_pharm_docs via find on pharmacy_billing with status != completed, sorted by billDate desc, limited to 5
- recent_pharmacy via find on pharmacy_billing sorted by billDate desc, limited to limit

4.4 How total revenue is calculated

Total revenue = paid invoice revenue + surgery revenue + completed pharmacy revenue

More precisely:
- Invoice contribution: sum of patients.billing.invoices.patientResponsibility where invoice status = paid
- Surgery contribution: sum of patients.billing.surgeryBills.patientTotalShare where surgery status is one of settled, balance_due, partially_paid
- Pharmacy contribution: sum of pharmacy_billing.totalAmount where status = completed

Important observation:
- Surgery revenue is counted even when not fully settled if status is balance_due or partially_paid.
- Therefore totalRevenue is not identical to cash collected.

4.5 How pending bills is calculated

Pending bills count = unpaid invoice count + balance-due surgery count + non-completed pharmacy bill count

More precisely:
- Invoices: count of patients.billing.invoices where status != paid
- Surgery: count of patients.billing.surgeryBills where status = balance_due
- Pharmacy: count of pharmacy_billing records where status != completed

Important observation:
- Surgery status partially_paid contributes to unsettledRevenue but not to pendingBills count.
- That means unsettled revenue and pending bill count are not aligned measures.

4.6 How unsettled bills is calculated

Unsettled revenue is calculated only from embedded surgery bills in patients.billing.surgeryBills.

Formula:
- sum patientTotalShare where surgery bill status in [balance_due, partially_paid]

Important observation:
- Unsettled bill does not include unpaid invoices.
- Unsettled bill does not include unpaid pharmacy bills.
- Unsettled bill does not include standalone final_surgery_bills or initial_surgery_bills.
- The dashboard's unsettled value is therefore a surgery-specific unsettled patient-share metric, not a universal outstanding-balance metric.

6. STEP 5 - DATA CONSISTENCY RISKS
====================================================================

Risk 1: invoice data has two storage models with no synchronization
- create_invoice writes patients.billing.invoices only.
- update_bill_with_dates writes billing_invoices only.
- get_patient_invoices reads only patients.billing.invoices.
- dashboard and analytics also read only patients.billing.invoices.
Impact:
- An invoice saved through update_bill_with_dates can exist in billing_invoices but remain invisible to patient invoice history, billing summary calculations, dashboard stats, and billing analytics.

Risk 2: surgery bills also have two storage models with no synchronization
- create_initial_surgery_bill and create_final_surgery_bill write patients.billing.surgeryBills.
- update_initial_bill_with_dates writes initial_surgery_bills only.
- update_final_bill_with_dates writes final_surgery_bills only.
- get_patient_surgery_bills and dashboard/analytics read only patients.billing.surgeryBills.
Impact:
- Dated surgery bills stored in standalone collections may never appear in patient surgery-bill history or dashboard revenue calculations.

Risk 3: pharmacy billing is dual-written with no reconciliation path
- create_pharmacy_bill writes pharmacy_billing and also pushes the same bill into patients.pharmacyBills.
- get_patient_pharmacy_bills reads pharmacy_billing only.
- dashboard stats and analytics read pharmacy_billing only.
- no endpoint reads patients.pharmacyBills for authoritative billing history.
Impact:
- Patient mirror can drift from collection truth.
- Patient document stores redundant billing copies with no visible consumption path in billing reads.

Risk 4: billing summary is incomplete relative to total billing state
- get_billing_summary reads only patients.billing.invoices and billing_cases active count.
- It does not include standalone billing_invoices.
- It does not include pharmacy_billing.
- It does not include standalone initial_surgery_bills/final_surgery_bills.
Impact:
- Patient summary can under-report actual billing exposure depending on which billing flow was used.

Risk 5: dashboard metrics are not source-of-truth metrics for all billing types
- unsettledRevenue uses only embedded surgery bills.
- pendingBills counts invoices, surgery balance_due, and non-completed pharmacy bills, but not standalone invoice/surgery collections.
Impact:
- Dashboard numbers depend on storage path rather than full business reality.

Risk 6: partially_paid surgery bills affect revenue but not pending count the same way
- totalRevenue includes balance_due and partially_paid surgery patient share.
- unsettledRevenue includes balance_due and partially_paid surgery patient share.
- pendingBills counts only balance_due surgery bills.
Impact:
- KPI definitions are internally inconsistent.

Risk 7: patient upsert on billing writes can create partial patient records
- create_invoice, create_initial_surgery_bill, create_final_surgery_bill, record_payment, create_claim use patient upsert behavior.
Impact:
- If registrationId does not already represent a complete patient record, billing writes may create minimal or malformed patient documents with only billing sections.

Risk 8: no standalone payment or claim collections
- payments and claims exist only in patient embedded arrays.
Impact:
- Finance auditability and reporting outside patient documents are harder to implement.
- Future hybrid migration would need new standalone storage or a strong patient-centric source-of-truth decision.

Risk 9: route namespace overlap hides different storage semantics
- POST /api/billing/patient/{registration_id}/surgery-bills/initial exists as both create_initial_surgery_bill and update_initial_bill_with_dates path shape family.
- POST /api/billing/patient/{registration_id}/surgery-bills/final exists similarly.
Impact:
- Similar route families are serving different persistence models, which increases integration confusion.

8. STEP 7 - FINAL VERIFICATION CONCLUSION
====================================================================

Billing write paths:
- Embedded patient writes: insurance, invoices, surgery bills, payments, claims.
- Standalone writes: billing_cases, billing_invoices, initial_surgery_bills, final_surgery_bills, pharmacy_billing, coupon_quotas.
- Dual-write path: pharmacy billing also mirrors to patients.pharmacyBills.

Billing read paths:
- Patient-level reads mostly use patients.billing arrays.
- Case reads use billing_cases.
- Pharmacy reads use pharmacy_billing.
- Dashboard and analytics use patients billing arrays plus pharmacy_billing.
- Standalone billing_invoices, initial_surgery_bills, and final_surgery_bills are not part of dashboard calculations.

Dashboard aggregation logic:
- Uses 9 Mongo queries total.
- Uses 7 aggregation pipelines and 2 standard queries.
- Revenue, pending bills, and unsettled bills are computed from mixed but incomplete billing sources.
- Unsettled bill specifically means surgery patient-share still due, not overall unpaid billing.

Hybrid billing status:
- Hybrid storage definitely exists.
- It is not unified.
- It has no clear source-of-truth contract.

Primary consistency risks:
- invoice split across patient arrays and billing_invoices
- surgery bill split across patient arrays and initial/final standalone collections
- pharmacy dual-write without reconciliation
- dashboard and summaries that ignore part of stored billing data

Most important file requiring future architectural work:
- backend/main.py

Bottom line:
The backend already behaves like a partially hybrid billing system, but it is not consistently implemented as one. The dominant operational source of truth today is still the patient document for invoices, surgery bills, payments, and claims, while standalone invoice and surgery bill collections are written by alternate endpoints that most billing reads do not consume. Any move to a fully hybrid design will require synchronized write logic and read-side reconciliation across nearly every billing handler in backend/main.py.
