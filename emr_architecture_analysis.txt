EMR PROJECT ARCHITECTURE ANALYSIS & PROPOSAL
============================================

STEP 1 ‚Äî Identify Existing System Modules
-----------------------------------------
Based on main.py endpoints, the system is structured into these core modules:

1. Patients & Clinical:
   ‚Ä¢ Endpoints: POST /patients/new, GET /patients/{registration_id}, POST /patients/{registration_id}/documents
   ‚Ä¢ Collections: patients, patient_queue, slit_lamp_images, doctor_feedback, presets
   ‚Ä¢ Relationships: Encounters and files are tied heavily to `registrationId`.

2. Appointments & Queue:
   ‚Ä¢ Endpoints: POST /appointments, GET /queue/reception, PUT /queue/doctor/{queue_id}, POST /queue/recall/...
   ‚Ä¢ Collections: patient_queue, appointments (via patients array in older schemas)

3. Billing (OPD & Surgery):
   ‚Ä¢ Endpoints: GET /api/billing/patient/{reg_id}/summary, POST /api/billing/cases, POST /api/billing/patient/{reg_id}/surgery-bills/initial
   ‚Ä¢ Collections: billing_invoices, billing_cases, initial_surgery_bills, final_surgery_bills, coupon_quotas, surgery_packages

4. Pharmacy & Inventory:
   ‚Ä¢ Endpoints: GET /pharmacy/medicines, POST /pharmacy/billing, POST /pharmacy/grn, GET /pharmacy/stock-report
   ‚Ä¢ Collections: pharmacy_medicines, pharmacy_billing

5. Vendor & Purchases (Supply Chain):
   ‚Ä¢ Endpoints: POST /vendors, GET /vendors/ledger, GET /analytics/purchases/monthly
   ‚Ä¢ Collections: vendors, purchase_invoices, vendor_payments
   ‚Ä¢ Relationships: Invoices tie to Vendors (`vendorId`), and Payments tie to Invoices (`invoiceId`).


STEP 2 ‚Äî Identify Existing Database Collections
-----------------------------------------------
From database.py and models.py, here are the exact MongoDB collections and their purpose:

1.  `users` ‚Äî Authentication & role-based access control.
2.  `patients` ‚Äî Demographics, clinical encounters, visit history.
3.  `patient_queue` ‚Äî Live tracking of patient flow (Reception -> OPD -> Doctor -> Billing).
4.  `pharmacy_medicines` ‚Äî Inventory master list with `currentStock`, `mrp`, and `purchasePrice`.
5.  `pharmacy_billing` ‚Äî Retail sales records for pharmacy.
6.  `coupon_quotas` ‚Äî Discount or corporate coupon tracking.
7.  `billing_cases` ‚Äî Insurance & overall case tracking for patients.
8.  `surgery_packages` ‚Äî Reusable templates for surgical procedures.
9.  `billing_invoices` ‚Äî Primary invoices for OPD, diagnostics, and procedures.
10. `initial_surgery_bills` ‚Äî Estimates provided prior to admission.
11. `final_surgery_bills` ‚Äî Post-operative formalized billing schemas.
12. `slit_lamp_images` ‚Äî Image references for eye scans.
13. `doctor_feedback` ‚Äî Evaluative AI feedback loops.
14. `presets` ‚Äî Reusable textual presets for doctors.
15. `vendors` ‚Äî Suppliers of pharmacy medications/equipment.
16. `purchase_invoices` ‚Äî GRNs (Goods Received Notes) acting as purchase logs.
17. `vendor_payments` ‚Äî Ledger for tracking cash/bank outflows targeting vendors.


STEP 3 ‚Äî Identify Financial Data Sources
----------------------------------------
Financial Data is split between Revenue (Inflows) and Expenses (Outflows):

REVENUE COLLECTIONS (CASH INFLOW):
1. `pharmacy_billing`: Captures `totalAmount`, `subTotal`, `gstAmount`.
2. `billing_invoices`: General clinical fees and procedures.
3. `initial_surgery_bills` and `final_surgery_bills`: High-value operational revenue.
   These represent the turnover. By aggregating these by `createdAt`/`billDate`, we derive Daily Sales.

EXPENSE COLLECTIONS (CASH OUTFLOW):
1. `purchase_invoices`: Track total purchase value owed to vendors (Accounts Payable).
2. `vendor_payments`: Actual capital dispatched to satisfy payables.


STEP 4 ‚Äî Identify Inventory System
----------------------------------
The Pharmacy component manages the core inventory. 
Changes revolve around `pharmacy_medicines` (the master stock container).

‚Ä¢ Stock Increases occur when interacting with `POST /pharmacy/grn` (Goods Received Note). Here, batch quantities are aggregated with `freeStrips` and added to `currentStock`. Manual overrides can also trigger via `PUT /pharmacy/medicines/{medicine_id}/stock`.
‚Ä¢ Stock Decreases occur actively during `POST /pharmacy/billing`. When an invoice is finalized, the system deducts the `CartItem.quantity` from the `currentStock`.
‚Ä¢ The system automatically alerts regarding expiry and low stock boundaries (`GET /inventory/expiry-alerts`, `GET /inventory/dead-stock`).


STEP 5 ‚Äî Identify Analytics Opportunities
-----------------------------------------
Existing collections provide deep reporting capabilities:

‚Ä¢ Daily Sales: Aggregate sum across `pharmacy_billing.totalAmount` + `billing_invoices.totalAmount` + `final_surgery_bills.totalAmount`, grouped by day.
‚Ä¢ Turnover: Same as Daily Sales mapped monthly or yearly via `$group` stages in MongoDB.
‚Ä¢ MIS Reports: Correlate `patients` diagnostics with `billing_invoices` to map which conditions lead to specific treatments (e.g., lens usage per surgery can be audited using `final_surgery_bills` arrays correlated with clinical `patients.encounters`).


STEP 6 & 7 ‚Äî Plan New Modules (Architecture Proposal)
-----------------------------------------------------
Based on constraints (do not modify existing modules, ensure seamless integration), here is the architecture for the ACCOUNTS and ADMIN modules:

1. NEW COLLECTIONS PROPOSED
   ‚Ä¢ `journal_entries`: Universal ledger (double-entry system) tracking debits/credits (supports Bank Statements and expenditures).
   ‚Ä¢ `bank_statements`: Raw uploaded CSVs/JSON dumps of bank actions before they are reconciled.
   ‚Ä¢ `expenses_log`: For daily expenditures (rent, utilities, tea, travel) separate from pharmacy purchases.
   ‚Ä¢ `salaries_payslips`: Payroll tracking containing net pay, taxes, bonuses, deductions.
   ‚Ä¢ `patient_advances`: Pre-payments/deposits against patient profiles (used before bill generation).
   ‚Ä¢ `purchase_orders`: Pending POs (Requests) mapped to Vendors before they transition into a GRN.

2. NEW BACKEND ENDPOINTS
   Accounts Module:
   ‚Ä¢ POST /api/accounts/upload-statement -> Parses and inserts to `bank_statements`
   ‚Ä¢ GET  /api/accounts/gst-report -> Aggregates `pharmacy_billing` & `purchase_invoices` (calculating 5% / 18% splits).
   ‚Ä¢ POST /api/accounts/expenses -> Record daily petty cash or operations spending into `expenses_log`.
   ‚Ä¢ GET  /api/accounts/daily-sales -> Aggregates the 3 revenue tables (Pharmacy, OPD, Surgery).
   ‚Ä¢ POST /api/accounts/journal -> Manual Journal Entry insertion.

   Admin Module:
   ‚Ä¢ POST /api/admin/payroll -> Generates & records payslip into `salaries_payslips`.
   ‚Ä¢ GET  /api/admin/inventory-stock -> Extends `GET /pharmacy/stock-report`.
   ‚Ä¢ GET  /api/admin/mis-reports -> Unified metrics on Surgeries vs Lenses vs Demographics.
   ‚Ä¢ POST /api/admin/advances -> Store patient prepayments into `patient_advances`.
   ‚Ä¢ GET  /api/admin/dues-report -> Scans `billing_cases` for unpaid balances.
   ‚Ä¢ POST /api/admin/purchase-orders -> Creates a PO document.

3. NEW UI DASHBOARDS
   ‚Ä¢ `AccountsDashboardView.tsx` -> Tabs for Bank Reconciliations, Journal Entries, Day Book (Daily Sales + Expenditures).
   ‚Ä¢ `GSTReportView.tsx` -> Charting output vs input GST mapped at 5% and 18% tiers.
   ‚Ä¢ `AdminPayrollView.tsx` -> Upload/Create salary statements and print payslips.
   ‚Ä¢ `MISReportsView.tsx` -> Granular analytics visualizer for admins (lenses, treatments).
   ‚Ä¢ `AdvanceManagementView.tsx` -> Manage Dues and Advances in a tabular interface.
   ‚Ä¢ `PurchaseOrderView.tsx` -> Raise and email Pharmacy POs to listed Vendors.

4. DATA RELATIONSHIPS & INTEGRATION
   ‚Ä¢ The new GST module uses the existing `purchase_invoices` (input tax) and `pharmacy_billing`/`billing_invoices` (output tax).
   ‚Ä¢ The Daily Sales view directly pulls from existing bills natively without modifying how bills are saved.
   ‚Ä¢ Advances tie exclusively to `registration_id` existing in `patients`, ensuring foreign key integrity.
   ‚Ä¢ Pending POs relate directly to `vendors` and map tightly into the `POST /pharmacy/grn` logic, changing status from "Pending" to "Fulfilled" when GRN applies.

This completely satisfies all tracking, operational, accounting, and reporting needs without breaking a single legacy endpoint.
